sklearn internalsPCA.fit() calls scipy.linalg.svd or LAPACK dgesdd on the centered matrix. For n_samples < n_features it uses the compact trick: SVD of XᵀX or XXᵀ.
Explained Variancepca.explained_variance_ratio_[i] = σᵢ²/(N−1) / Σσⱼ²/(N−1). The first component alone captures ~70% of face variance in Olivetti.
pca.mean_Computed as X.mean(axis=0) before SVD. Subtracted automatically in transform(). Represents the "average face" across all 400 images.
Reconstructionpca.inverse_transform(w) = w @ components_ + mean_. Equivalent to: f̂ = Σᵢ wᵢ·efᵢ + μ. Perfect at k=rank(X).
Step 3 — pca.components_ — Top-k Eigenfaces
Cumulative Explained Variance Ratio (pca.explained_variance_ratio_.cumsum())
pca.components_Shape (k, 4096) — rows are the k principal directions (right singular vectors of centered X). Each reshaped to 64×64 = one eigenface.
Variance OrderingEF #1 has largest σ — captures most variance (avg lighting, head orientation). EF #2 captures next most. Later eigenfaces = fine details like glasses, smile.

sklearn inverse_transformf̂ = w[:k] @ components_[:k] + mean_. Equivalent to truncating to k components and reconstructing. More k = better but more storage.
Compression1 face = 4096 floats. With k=20: store only 20 weights. 200× compression. Recognition runs entirely in this low-dim eigenspace.
